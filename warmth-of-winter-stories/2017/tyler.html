Tyler

We met Tyler at the corner of Howe st and Robson st. He came all the way from Ontario with his dog, Lokey. He’s had him for 3 years since he was a puppy. It took them about two weeks to get to Vancouver from Ontario through hitch hiking. He says even though it is much wetter in Vancouver he likes it here better than Ontario because it’s warmer and people are nice.

Christine's Reflection

It was a little difficult approaching people at first because I was not sure whether they would be willing to talk to us or not. However, after about third time, I felt more natural talking to them and asking them questions about their journey and life in Vancouver.

Tyler did not talk much and he apologized for being shy but I could tell that he had a big heart from the way he looked at his dog and how he truly loved him.

One of the big things I realized from participating in Warmth of Winter was how easy it was for us to just pack some food and distribute to the Vancouver East side community. I plan on gathering a couple of my friends to create our own “Warmth of Winter” group and go down there more often so I can build more of a long-term relationship with them. One of the downsides was that a lot of them were not willing to share their personal stories with us which is understandable since we had just met them so I think visiting them on regular basis and building a relationship with them would help us identify their real situation and their needs.
