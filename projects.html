Warmth of Winter

During the winter months H.O.P.E. gifted blankets to homeless members of Vancouver’s downtown east side. However, the purpose of this initiative was not only to provide these necessary supplies but also to engage in meaningful conversations. In this initiative, the goal was to learn about the ambitions, dreams, and stories of our Vancouver neighbours whose voices are often silenced by societal norms.

HOPE for Success

At this workshop, students learn about the application process to post-secondary institutions around the world as well as tips and tricks on how to improve their applications for the admission season. Following this, future workshops are targeted towards scholarships, interviews, and finally acceptances and planning for university careers.

Dream It Be It

HOPE brought together a vast array of speakers and community change makers from different walks of life in efforts to empower, educate, and equip youth of this generation with the tools and skills needed to turn their dreams into reality. This conference served as a launching pad into potential mentorship opportunities, internships, and job-shadowing opportunities with speakers and guests.

Hope for Homes

The first part of the project ‘Clothing for the Community’ collects clothing from a school-wide clothing drive to go towards Covenant House Vancouver. The second part of the project, ‘Hampers for the Holidays’, enlists the support of local stores to donate basic necessities to create care packages for those living outside in the winter.

Life Skills Outreach

The life skills outreach paired high school students from grades eight through twelve with students with behavioural and developmental disabilities in the life skills program. These student pairings engaged in pen pal programs, full day workshops and outings together to foster a stronger bond and to push the boundaries on societal perception and stereotypes.

Women's Career Conference

UBC HOPE and SI of Vancouver Women's Career Conference brought together women from different walks of life in efforts to empower, educate and equip women of this generation with the tools and skills needed to turn their dreams into reality. They will learn important life skills such as university transition and how to network with professionals in the field they hope to go into.

Summer Workshop

To help incoming first year students transition from high school to university, UBC H.O.P.E. offered a four part workshop throughout the summer months. The Campus Tour workshop bought students all over campus where they were put into small groups of maximum 5 students so that they can have more personalized attention and get all their questions answered.

Free The Children

The story of H.O.P.E.’s creation and purpose was recently introduced on the international stage at We Day Vancouver 2014. H.O.P.E.’s Life Skills Outreach pen pal program was also featured in Voices International, a quarterly online magazine by Free the Children that highlights outstanding initiatives young people are engaging in around the world.

Media and The Mind

HOPE, Alberta STAND, and Soroptimist International organizations of Vancouver and Edmonton were proud to present the Media and the Mind Conference on May 14th, 2016. This year’s conference is focused on bringing together members of the community to create an open dialogue regarding mental health as well as how young people of this generation perceive and use social media.
